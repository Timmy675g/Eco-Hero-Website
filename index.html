Frequently Asked Questions
Climate Change

1. Is Climate Change natural or human made?

Both. Natural can cause climate changes by Earth's itself changing orbit and volcanic eruptions can cause climate change. Humans also causes climate change through deforestation, burning fossil fuels, and industrial activities, which speed up the process.

2. What are the main causes of climate change?

The main causes include burning fossil fuels for electricity and heat, deforestation, industrial activities, transportation emissions, and agricultural practices that release greenhouse gases.

3. What are the effects of climate change?

Effects include rising sea levels, extreme weather events, melting glaciers, loss of biodiversity, threats to food security, and displacement of communities in vulnerable regions.

4. How does climate change affect humans, animals, and plants?

Climate change can cause increased heat waves, rising temperatures for us, extreme and unpredictable weathers which can destroy natural habitats and air pollution can cause heart problems and cancer.

5. What is Carbon Footprint and what is the effect?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases, like carbon dioxide and methane ( Greenhouse Gas), released into the atmosphere by human activities.
The effects is rising temperatures, pollution, ecosystem disruption and extreme weather events.

6. Does Recycling really works for climate change?

Yes, recycling makes a significant difference for climate change by reducing greenhouse gas emissions, primarily by saving energy and decreasing the need to extract and process natural materials
